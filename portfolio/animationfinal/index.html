COMS W4167: Computer Animation - Fall 2014, Final Project

Columbia University, The Fu Foundation School of Engineering and Applied Science

Student: Danilo Faria (df2553)

For this project I implemented the physics for simple gravity, gravitational force, spring force, buoyancy force and radial buoyancy force all in three dimensions. I also coded 5 different interactive experiences ("scenes") that make use of the aforementioned forces. I used the Three.js library to render the scenes, but I coded all of the physics and program logic from scratch. This project is my first experience with Three.js.

Scenes

Scene One

Solar System

A simple model of our Solar System. Utilizes gravitational forces that I've implemented in javascript based on our previous course experience. All planets are to scale with each other in size but not in distances (however, the sun is not).

View Scene

Scene Two

Gravity and Spring

In this scene you can see several "planets" going in random directions around a center body. The center body consists of a bunch of particles connected by springs to one central particle. Spring force was also implemented based on previous course experience.

View Scene

Scene Three

Springs

Here you can see several particles connected by springs in a way to lead to some interesting motion.

View Scene

Scene Four

Buoyancy

Several balls floating on water because of buoyancy forces that balance out gravity. User is able to inflate or deflate the balls while maintaining their weights, such that the balls will float or sink accordingly. Buoyancy force was implemented based on the book Game Physics Engine Development. However, the book only considers the case of an surface on the ZX plane and my implementation generalizes for any plane defined by a point and a normal vector.

View Scene

Scene Five

Radial Buoyancy

A simulation of a planet covered in liquid while attracting several other bodies in its direction through gravity until the bodies begin to float on the surface of the planet. Like in the previous scene, the user can control the volume of the balls and make them sink or float. For this I implemented radial buoyancy force that considers the surface to be some sphere with a defined radius.
