Brainoi Instructions

The Brainoi puzzle is made up of three grid-like compartments, each with a certain height and certain width, and blocks of different widths inside of these compartments, possibly stacked. Each block can only be on top of a larger block or on the bottom of a compartment. To complete the level, you must move each block such that eventually all of the blocks are inside of the rightmost compartment.

Each move has to obey the following simple rules:

  • Only one block can be moved at a time.
  • Each block can only be moved if it is free of blocks on top of itself.
  • No block may be placed on top of smaller blocks or blocks of the same size.

About Brainoi

The Brainoi puzzle is a brand-new problem whose mechanics are similar to those of the Tower of Hanoi problem. It was created by computer science student Danilo Faria and explored by him in his undergraduate thesis. Link to thesis summary: bit.ly/brainoi2014.
